As described in the Hypothesis Testing part of the course: The p-value is the probability of getting our statistic or a more extreme value if the null is true.
Therefore, small p-values suggest our null is not true. Rather, our statistic is likely to have come from a dierent distribution than the null. When the p-value is
large, we have evidence that our statistic was likely to come from the null hypothesis. Therefore, we do not have evidence to reject the null. By comparing our
p-value to our type I error threshold (\alpha), we can make our decision about which hypothesis we will choose.
